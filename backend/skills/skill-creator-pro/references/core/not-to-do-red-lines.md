# Not-To-Do Red Lines

These red lines combine the whitepaper anti-patterns with the governance lessons from recent upgrades.

1. Do not restructure a skill before choosing its primary paradigm.
2. Do not hide trigger logic in the body when it belongs in frontmatter.
3. Do not turn `SKILL.md` into a textbook, tutorial, or encyclopedia.
4. Do not copy large reference content back into the control layer.
5. Do not create empty or decorative directories.
6. Do not guess the environment when recon should happen first.
7. Do not use vague adjectives where an execution rule or threshold is needed.
8. Do not do a full rewrite when a surgical edit is enough.
9. Do not declare success without review, validation, or a realistic usage-path check.
10. Do not keep pushing changes just to clear low-value review hints if readability gets worse.

## Whitepaper Failure Classes
- Information failure: main file too long, basic explanations, poor layering.
- Behavior failure: no decision tree, no recon, no validation.
- Governance failure: no interruption points, no review lens, no anti-pattern guardrails.
